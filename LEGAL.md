# Legal Status and Compliance Guardrails

## Legal entity status

Karma Infinity is operated as Karma Infinity LLC, a New Jersey limited liability company.

Karma Infinity LLC is not a nonprofit organization and must not be described as a nonprofit, charity, charitable organization, tax-exempt organization, foundation, or 501(c)(3) unless that status is formally created, approved, documented, and reflected in the repository by maintainers.

## Default legal assumption

For all repository work, documentation, funding language, public messaging, issue templates, product plans, and contributor workflows, assume:

```text
Karma Infinity LLC = New Jersey limited liability company
Karma Infinity LLC != nonprofit
Karma Infinity LLC != 501(c)(3)
Karma Infinity LLC != tax-exempt charitable organization
```

## Funding language rules

Use these terms when accurate:

- Funding
- Sponsorship
- Support
- Customer payment
- Partnership
- Revenue
- Grant, only when the grant program allows LLC or for-profit eligibility
- Investment, only when legally appropriate and reviewed

Avoid these terms unless reviewed and legally accurate:

- Donation
- Charitable donation
- Tax-deductible donation
- Nonprofit donation
- Charity contribution
- 501(c)(3) contribution
- Fiscal sponsorship, unless a valid fiscal sponsor relationship exists

## Tax-deductibility rule

Do not state or imply that payments to Karma Infinity LLC are tax-deductible charitable contributions.

If funding language is needed, use neutral wording such as:

```text
Karma Infinity LLC may accept sponsorships, commercial payments, partnership funding, or other lawful support. Payments to Karma Infinity LLC should not be described as tax-deductible charitable donations unless a qualified legal structure is formally established and documented.
```

## Grants and public funding

Before applying for or referencing grants, confirm whether the program allows:

- For-profit LLC applicants
- New Jersey LLC applicants
- Small business applicants
- Social enterprise applicants
- Fiscal-sponsor arrangements
- Nonprofit-only applicants

If a grant is nonprofit-only, do not present Karma Infinity LLC as eligible unless an approved legal pathway exists.

## Public claims and mission language

Karma Infinity may describe its mission, impact goals, ethical commitments, and public-benefit intentions.

However, public-benefit language must not imply nonprofit, charitable, tax-exempt, or 501(c)(3) status.

Preferred phrasing:

```text
Karma Infinity LLC is a mission-driven New Jersey limited liability company building ethical technology for human dignity, resilience, and long-term community benefit.
```

Avoid phrasing:

```text
Karma Infinity is a nonprofit charity.
Donations are tax deductible.
Give a charitable contribution to Karma Infinity.
Karma Infinity is a 501(c)(3).
```

## Legal review triggers

Escalate for legal or qualified professional review before publishing, merging, or deploying content involving:

- Fundraising language
- Grants
- Investor language
- Equity, tokens, revenue share, or securities-like structures
- Tax-deductibility claims
- Public benefit or charitable claims
- Health, mental health, legal, crisis, or financial advice
- User data collection or sensitive personal information
- Partnerships with schools, clinics, governments, nonprofits, or vulnerable-population programs
- Terms of service, privacy policy, consent language, disclaimers, or contracts

## Repository checklist

Every material repository change should ask:

- Does this accidentally call Karma Infinity a nonprofit?
- Does this imply 501(c)(3), charity, tax-exempt, or public charity status?
- Does this call payments donations when they should be sponsorships, funding, payments, or support?
- Does this imply tax deductibility?
- Does this create grant, investor, user-data, health, legal, or financial compliance risk?
- Does this need review before publication?

## Maintainer rule

If there is uncertainty, do not guess. Use neutral LLC-safe language and flag the item for review.

This file is an operational guardrail, not legal advice. Karma Infinity LLC should consult qualified counsel or tax professionals for entity, tax, fundraising, securities, nonprofit, and regulatory questions.
