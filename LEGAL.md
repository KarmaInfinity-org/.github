# Legal Status and Compliance Guardrails

## Legal entity status

Karma Infinity is operated as Karma Infinity LLC, a New Jersey limited liability company.

Karma Infinity LLC is not a nonprofit organization and must not be described as a nonprofit, charity, charitable organization, tax-exempt organization, foundation, public charity, or 501(c)(3) unless that status is formally created, approved, documented, and reflected in the repository by maintainers.

## Current compliance checkpoints

Last researched: 2026-05-01

These checkpoints are included so repo work does not drift into outdated or legally inaccurate assumptions.

### New Jersey annual report

New Jersey businesses must file annual reports. Business.NJ.gov states that every business in New Jersey must file an annual report, that the filing includes keeping registered-agent and address information current, and that the annual report is due every year on the last day of the month in which the business was formed.

Repository implication: do not represent Karma Infinity LLC as inactive, informal, unregistered, or entity-agnostic. Operational planning should include annual-report and registered-agent maintenance.

### New Jersey registration and public funding readiness

The New Jersey Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services states that companies doing business in New Jersey must be registered, including businesses seeking to contract with a public agency or applying for a State grant or tax credit.

Repository implication: grant, public-contracting, tax-credit, certification, or government-partnership language should be reviewed for eligibility and registration requirements before publication or submission.

### Federal BOI reporting status

FinCEN currently states that entities created in the United States, including entities previously known as domestic reporting companies, and their beneficial owners are exempt from the requirement to report beneficial ownership information to FinCEN under the Corporate Transparency Act. FinCEN says this change comes from an interim final rule published March 26, 2025.

Repository implication: do not add an automatic BOI filing requirement for Karma Infinity LLC unless this is re-verified against current FinCEN guidance. Because BOI rules have changed and remain sensitive, re-check FinCEN before making compliance statements.

### IRS charitable contribution status

The IRS states that charitable contribution deductions generally require contributions to be made to qualifying organizations, and IRS 501(c)(3) guidance says 501(c)(3) organizations are commonly referred to as charitable organizations and are eligible to receive tax-deductible contributions under Code section 170.

Repository implication: do not state or imply that payments, sponsorships, support, funding, grants, or purchases involving Karma Infinity LLC are tax-deductible charitable contributions unless a qualified legal structure is formally established and documented.

### New Jersey charitable solicitation risk

New Jersey charity guidance and enforcement materials show that organizations soliciting charitable donations or contributions in New Jersey may trigger Charities Registration and Investigation Act and Consumer Fraud Act obligations. New Jersey has also taken action where organizations solicited charitable contributions without proper registration or made misleading charitable claims.

Repository implication: avoid donation, charitable appeal, charity drive, tax-deductible contribution, nonprofit campaign, disaster-relief charity, or similar wording unless reviewed by qualified counsel and supported by the correct registration or exemption pathway.

## Funding language rules

Use these terms when accurate:

- Funding
- Sponsorship
- Support
- Customer payment
- Partnership
- Revenue
- Eligible grant funding, only when the grant program allows LLC, for-profit, small-business, or social-enterprise eligibility
- Investment, only when legally appropriate and reviewed

Avoid these terms unless reviewed and legally accurate:

- Donation
- Charitable donation
- Tax-deductible donation
- Nonprofit donation
- Charity contribution
- 501(c)(3) contribution
- Public charity contribution
- Foundation donation
- Fiscal sponsorship, unless a valid fiscal sponsor relationship exists

## Tax-deductibility rule

Do not state or imply that payments to Karma Infinity LLC are tax-deductible charitable contributions.

If funding language is needed, use neutral wording such as:

```text
Karma Infinity LLC may accept sponsorships, commercial payments, partnership funding, eligible grant funding, or other lawful support. Payments to Karma Infinity LLC should not be described as tax-deductible charitable donations unless a qualified legal structure is formally established and documented.
```

## Grants and public funding

Before applying for or referencing grants, confirm whether the program allows:

- For-profit LLC applicants
- New Jersey LLC applicants
- Small business applicants
- Social enterprise applicants
- Fiscal-sponsor arrangements
- Nonprofit-only applicants
- Required New Jersey registrations, certificates, tax status, or business certifications

If a grant is nonprofit-only, do not present Karma Infinity LLC as eligible unless an approved legal pathway exists.

## Public claims and mission language

Karma Infinity may describe its mission, impact goals, ethical commitments, and public-benefit intentions.

However, public-benefit language must not imply nonprofit, charitable, tax-exempt, public charity, foundation, or 501(c)(3) status.

Preferred phrasing:

```text
Karma Infinity LLC is a mission-driven New Jersey limited liability company building ethical technology for human dignity, resilience, and long-term community benefit.
```

Avoid phrasing:

```text
Karma Infinity is a nonprofit charity.
Donations are tax deductible.
Give a charitable contribution to Karma Infinity.
Karma Infinity is a 501(c)(3).
Karma Infinity is a public charity.
Karma Infinity accepts tax-deductible donations.
```
