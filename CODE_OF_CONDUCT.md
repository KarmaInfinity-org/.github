# Karma Infinity Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Karma Infinity is committed to maintaining a respectful, safe, inclusive, and mission-aligned environment for everyone who participates in our projects, discussions, repositories, events, documentation, and community spaces.

We are building systems intended to serve people. The way we build must reflect the dignity, care, and responsibility we want our technology to carry into the world.

## Our standard

Participants are expected to:

- Treat others with dignity and respect
- Communicate with patience, clarity, and good faith
- Welcome people across backgrounds, abilities, cultures, identities, and skill levels
- Focus feedback on ideas, designs, code, documentation, and outcomes rather than personal attacks
- Respect boundaries, privacy, and lived experience
- Admit mistakes and correct them openly
- Prioritize safety, accessibility, and human benefit
- Avoid inflammatory, discriminatory, exploitative, or dehumanizing language

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable in Karma Infinity spaces:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or threats
- Hate speech, slurs, or demeaning language
- Sexualized comments or unwanted sexual attention
- Publishing private information without consent
- Trolling, personal attacks, or deliberate disruption
- Abuse of power, access, credentials, or moderation authority
- Repeated bad-faith arguments after maintainers have asked for a reset
- Encouraging harm, exploitation, discrimination, or unsafe deployment of technology

## Mission-aligned collaboration

Karma Infinity is neutral, pro-human, and service-oriented.

We do not use project spaces to promote partisan conflict, ideological hostility, personal fame-seeking, or division. Contributors may come from many worldviews. Our shared ground is the work of building systems that reduce suffering, increase access, strengthen resilience, and protect human dignity.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they believe is necessary and proportionate to protect the community, including:

- Giving a private warning
- Requesting edits or behavior changes
- Temporarily limiting participation
- Closing or locking issues, pull requests, or discussions
- Removing content
- Revoking contributor access
- Banning a participant from project spaces

Enforcement decisions should be documented privately by maintainers when appropriate.

## Reporting

To report a concern, contact the maintainers through the support or governance pathway listed in `SUPPORT.md`.

For security vulnerabilities, do not open a public issue. Follow `SECURITY.md`.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Karma Infinity repositories, discussions, issues, pull requests, documentation spaces, and community channels when someone is participating as part of the Karma Infinity ecosystem.

## Review cycle

This policy should be reviewed at least twice per year as Karma Infinity grows.
